Multidisciplinary mechatronics expertise

Combining mechanical design, embedded systems, and control theory to build robots and automated systems that operate reliably in real-world, industrial, and research environments.

Designing, building, and controlling robotic systems from concept to deployment

From ROS-based navigation on a semi-autonomous hospital service robot to vision-guided SCARA manipulators and industrial PLC/HMI automation, this work spans the full robotics stack: mechanical design, sensing, control, and software integration.

  • ROS-based navigation with ±30 cm accuracy for a semi-autonomous medical service robot.
  • Vision-based screw detection (88% accuracy) and disturbance-observer control for a 4 DOF SCARA robot.
  • PLC/HMI-driven automation systems deployed on live production lines at MAS Holdings.

Research at Virginia Tech extends this into acoustically driven robotics — using membrane vibration and ultrasound actuation to propel mini-robots and manipulate bio-samples without direct mechanical contact.

Tools and platforms used across these projects include ROS, MATLAB/Simulink, COMSOL Multiphysics, SOLIDWORKS, and microcontrollers such as Jetson Nano, Raspberry Pi, Arduino, and STM32.
